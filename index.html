Ocean Acidification and Climate Change

By: Riddhi Bhoskar, Kartika Chavan, Sayli Dhotre

Introduction

What is Ocean Acidification? It is a process where increased atmospheric CO2 leads to lower pH in oceans.

What is Climate Change? Long-term changes in temperature and weather patterns due to human activities.

Importance: Both ocean acidification and climate change significantly impact marine ecosystems and human economies.

Causes

The burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, and natural gas) releases significant amounts of CO2 into the atmosphere. Deforestation and land-use changes also contribute to rising CO2 levels.

Chemical Process: When CO2 dissolves in seawater, it undergoes reactions that form carbonic acid and lower ocean pH.

Impact on Marine Life

Fish & Other Species

Acidification affects fish behavior, reproduction, and predator-prey interactions, potentially leading to population declines.

Biodiversity

Disruptions in coral, shellfish, and plankton populations affect the marine food web, leading to biodiversity loss.

Ecosystem Services

Healthy marine ecosystems provide carbon sequestration, coastal protection, and tourism, all of which are at risk.

Impact on Fisheries & Society

Declines in fish stocks due to ocean acidification pose risks to food security, livelihoods, and coastal economies.

Solutions

Reduce CO2 Emissions

Shift to renewable energy, improve energy efficiency, and adopt carbon pricing policies.

Restore Coastal Habitats

Mangroves and wetlands can absorb CO2 and protect coastal areas.

Protect Marine Areas

Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) can safeguard vulnerable ecosystems.

Use Technology

Invest in carbon capture and explore techniques like ocean alkalinity enhancement.
